I'm Brooke. I'm a PhD student in sociology at Brown University. My research interests are in environmental sociology, political economy, economic sociology, social movements, and social theory. I spend most of my time thinking about public power, municipal finance, and the intersection of psychoanalysis and sociology.

Before coming to Brown, I attempted a short-lived career as an environmental planner and policy analyst as I completed a Master in City Planning, with a concentration in environmental policy and planning, at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

My first, actual career was as a mechanical engineer. After a Bachelor's degree in mechanical engineering, which I completed at Cornell University in 2020, I worked as a mechanical engineer on hardware systems at a start-up in New Jersey, then in Klavs F. Jensen's lab in the department of chemical engineering at M.I.T., where I worked on several projects related to automated molecular discovery and reaction optimization. I came out of this role with roughly the same amount of chemical-engineering knowledge as I had when I entered. I am always happy to answer questions about mechanical engineering. (I am less happy to, but will, answer questions on how to transition from engineering to social science.)

In my free time, I like: writing poetry, going to jazz bars, snowboarding, yelling at rowers on the river (as a coxswain, and as a spectator), reading German modernist literature and psychoanalytic theory, watching Edward Yang films, attempting cryptic crossword puzzles, and sewing clothes that look like sacks.
